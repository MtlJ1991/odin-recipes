Braised Pork

The tender, succulent pork belly is braised in a fragrant sauce
infused with ginger, green onion, and warm spices like star anise
and cinnamon, creating a deeply satisfying and memorable meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs pork belly, cut into 2-inch cubes
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece ginger, sliced
  • 3 green onions, chopped
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup dark soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup Shaoxing wine (or dry sherry)
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 2 cups water or chicken broth
  • 2 star anise pods
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add the pork belly cubes and brown them on all sides. Remove the pork and set aside.
  2. In the same pot, add the garlic, ginger, and green onions. Saut√© for 2-3 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add the soy sauce, dark soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, and sugar to the pot. Stir to combine.
  4. Return the browned pork belly to the pot. Add the water or chicken broth, star anise pods, and cinnamon stick. Bring to a boil.
  5. Reduce the heat to low, cover, and let it simmer for about 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the pork is tender and the sauce has thickened. Stir occasionally.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Remove the star anise and cinnamon stick before serving.
  7. Serve the braised pork over steamed rice or with steamed buns. Enjoy!
